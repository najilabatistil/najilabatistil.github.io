Discover what a computer should be.

Introduced in 1984, the Macintosh revolutionized personal computing with its GUI, mouse, and compact design. Here are a few key features that made it a game-changer.

Say hello to the Macintosh.

Introduced in 1984, the Macintosh was one of the first widely available personal computers to feature a graphical user interface (GUI) and a mouse.

Before the Macintosh, computers were complex to use.

The Macintosh popularized the mouse, allowing users to interact with computers easily.

Store brilliance in your pocket.

The Macintosh used 3.5-inch floppy disks for storage, offering a portable way to save and share files compared to larger, earlier disk formats.

Point. Click.

The Macintosh made computers easy to use with its point-and-click interface.

Copy. Paste.

Macintosh had a copy-paste feature, which made it easier to move data between applications.

Type. Print.

The Macintosh made it easy to create, edit, and print documents.

Work blindingly fast.

The Macintosh allowed users to complete tasks more quickly compared to the text-based systems of the time.

Take Apple on the go.

The Macintosh could be easily carried in a bag.

Own your space. Own your work.

The Macintosh was designed with the individual user in mind.

If you can point, you can use a Macintosh.

Macintosh — The computer for the bemused, the confused, the intimidated.
